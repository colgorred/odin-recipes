Traditional Scotch Pancakes

A quick and easy breakfast that the whole family will love

Light and fluffy Scotch pancakes (also known as drop scones) are the perfect teatime treat or an easy weekend breakfast. Ready in just 20 minutes. Serve warm or cold, plain or topped with butter and jam.

Ingredients

  • 200g plain flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 tbsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 large egg
  • 300ml milk
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • vegetable oil, to fry

Steps

  1. Combine the vingar and milk, leaving at room temperature for at least 15 mins.
  2. Add the dry ingredients to a mixing bowl. Beat the egg and soured milk into the mixture with a handheld or electric whisk. Heat a non-stick frying pan over a medium heat and add some oil.
  3. Spoon in a small ladleful of batter to make a pancake about 10cm across. Fry for about 1 min until the edges of the pancake are firm and bubbles have started to appear on the surface. Turn over and fry for another 30 secs till golden on both sides. Serve with your favourite toppings.
